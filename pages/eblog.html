January 16, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #10

With the long weekend only a day away, the Iron Panthers focused their efforts on continuing the great job they have been doing all week.

With the prototype of the robot coming along nicely, the engineering team focused on designing specific parts of the robot. Some members of the team, led by Connor, focused on designing a mechanism that can lift the recycling bins and place them on top of the gray totes. Cuyler plugged and chugged offset for the support pillars in order to find which sprockets can be used while Reuben led a small group that worked on milling an extrude holder.

Programming continued to work hard on the source code. Leon led the group through organizing their code base and importing everyone's preliminary sources together. The programmers worked hard on finishing their classes so that they can eventually all be compiled and unit tested.

January 15, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #9

Programming worked on individual classes (individual tasks) in which they arecoding different components of the code for the robot. These classes included the three motor groups for the drive base, an action class, buttons, robot location, and event relay for the joystick.

Peter and Leon started planning out the autonomous code; they would ideally like to complete every task possible within the 15 seconds. They have two ideas: using a pre-programmed code so the robot can drive around or using Artificial Intelligence so the robot can decide its own pathway during the competition.

Engineering faced some difficulties today when many of their files on SolidWorkswere lost. They spent time trying to restructure the files and recreate them if needed. In addition, they continued to mill parts and cut wood for the tote stacking mechanism.

January 14, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #8

Wednesday late night sessions: intense, productive, and energetic.

Today, programming worked on testing the SmartDashboard on the roboRIO along with beginning efforts on the JoystickWrapper, EventRelay, Logger, Button, and Action classes designed by Peter and Leon. Early on, both programmers and engineers ran into a problem with the electronics test bed when a ferrule connector got stuck in a Weidmuller connector of the Power Distribution Panel. The connector snapped while trying to pull the wire out. After some time, the ferrule was successfuly extracted.

Engineers dedicated time today to finalizing the tote chute in which totes are loaded by a human player and released using a sliding panel onto the playing field. They have also started to work on the scoring area for the practice playing field. In addition, the engineers have made progress on the prototype of the conveyor belt for raising totes for stacking. They have constructed an eight foot tall prototype of the conveyor belt utilizing wood, string, and 2 x 4s. The cylinder that enables the conveyor belt to turn gets stuck easily. A possible solution is to use chain instead of a conveyor belt, although similar problems may arise.

Business developed a t-shirt design for the FRC Recycle Rush Challenge and were able to come up with a finalized idea. They also continued to work on the Business Plan and Chairman’s Award Video. Plans for two fundraisers within the next month – the Valentine’s Day Movie Night and Bag and Tag Dinner – are also moving forward.

Wednesdays are a wonderful day of the week; they not only work extremely hard and make strides with their projects, but also get to spend quality time together and grow together as a team.

January 13, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #7

This Tuesday was another day of the Iron Panthers hard at work on continuing their learning and projects for this build season. Today, the programming team split into several groups where programmers taught each other newly learned skills, advanced their knowledge in the field of sensors, and brainstormed the overall design of the code for the season. January 12, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #6

After a refreshing break on Sunday, the Iron Panthers returned to school rejuvenated and ready to tackle the busy week ahead. Programmer June is taking on the arduous task of cataloguing all of our electronics while the rest of programming is experimenting with new electronics. The D-Link router, the Axis M1013 camera (used for detecting items with reflective tape) and Anderson connectors (which make connecting and disconnecting wires and motor controllers easier) are the hot ticket items for today’s work session. Programmers also plan on using a circular green LED light array to make it easier for the Axis camera to find the reflective tape.

The engineers are playing around with the prototype that they built during the weekend. Three new members are working with the prototype and looking at possible lift mechanisms such as using a rope and hook. Additional members are continuing efforts with the human player chute.

January 10, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #5 Weekend

Over the weekend, the robotics team convened to continue their many projects associated with build season. Programming tested all of their electronics and was able to get their motor running! Additionally, they created a Joystick Wrapper – a catchall for functions, buttons, and tasks associated with the joystick. Leads Peter and Leon have almost completed this and plan to pass it on to the newer programmers so they can write the bulk of the code and gain more experience.

Engineers were able to get a better sense of the drive base for the robot. They set up gearboxes and motors. Another endeavor that they completed was the eight foot tall prototype of the lifting mechanism frame. After running out of lumber, they purchased more wood and started work on the tote chute for the human player component of Recycle Rush.

All in all it was a very successful meeting and the Iron Panthers feel even more prepared for Recycle Rush after this Saturday work day!.

January 9, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #4

Today's Friday afternoon was relatively relaxed. To close off the week, the engineering team analyzed and planned for the drive base system. As of yet, fortunately, no issues have arisen in the current planning and prototyping phase. January 7, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #2

Planning and preparation for Recycle Rush continues today as the Robotics Team meets for their late night Wednesday work sessions. In these five hours, the programmers and engineers have time to really delve into the nitty gritty details of the game and trouble shoot any problems they encounter. Moreover, the team is able to utilize the lab for an extended period of time with the help of mentors who arrive after they have finished work.

Engineering is dedicating time to learning about different methods for picking up totes: the building blocks for the towers that the robot must build during competition. They began by hosting a round table discussion of various tote mechanisms, then transitioned into watching “Robot in 3 Days” videos in which teams build robots for the FRC challenge within 3 days. These videos gave the engineering team some jumpstarting points for developing their tote chute. Ideally, the chute will be used to hold totes that the human player places inside.

While Engineering is hard at work, programmers are expanding on their plan for how to organize and manage code this season. Leads Leon and Peter led a tutorial on how they envision coding for Recycle Rush; most of the programming team attended so they will be prepared for the remainder of build season. In addition, programmers will begin to experiment with the different parts of the roboRio program, an extremely compact and powerful controlling system.

January 6, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #1

The last few days of winter break were busy for the Iron Panthers. Following kickoff at Woodside High, the engineers and programmers shuttled off to the house of Engineering Lead, Reuben Grossman to read and discuss the rules. Reading each and every section of the game manual out loud, the team made sure to acquaint themselves with the specifics of the game so they could be prepared for competitions this season.

Monday was another day off for BHS students, but not for the Iron Panthers! Engineers and programmers met to review the rules once more and start discussing the merits of various strategies. They concurred that moving the totes would be the key part of the robot’s functions. On the other hand, using a cost benefit analysis, they reached the conclusion that picking up the pool noodles “litter” would not be advantageous.

Today is Tuesday, and the team is beginning to start developing a game plan for tackling Recycle Rush. Programmers are outlining how they are going to design and allocate the code, as well as coordinate which team members will work on specific systems. Engineers are making sure that all members have read the rules.
